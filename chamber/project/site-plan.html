WorkLink Site Plan

Site Name

Proposed Site Name: WorkLink

Reason for Selection: The name "WorkLink" was chosen to clearly communicate the site's primary function: linking job seekers (employees) with employers. It is a professional, memorable, and available domain name that is easy to spell and pronounce. The name suggests connection, efficiency, and a bridge between two parties in the professional world.

Site Purpose

The purpose of WorkLink is to create a dynamic, trustworthy platform for employers and employees to connect and collaborate. The site will provide tools for employers to post job openings and for job seekers to showcase their skills and experience. A key feature will be a two-way rating and review system, allowing both parties to rate their experience with each other, fostering a community of reliable professionals and companies. The site's goal is to increase transparency and accountability in the job market, helping users make more informed decisions.

Scenarios

  • Scenario 1 (Employer): "I am a small business owner looking for a freelance web developer for a short-term project. I'm new to hiring online and want to find someone reliable. How can I easily filter candidates by their project completion rates and read reviews from other employers to ensure I hire a trustworthy professional?"
  • Scenario 2 (Employee): "I'm a freelance graphic designer looking for new contract work. I've had bad experiences with companies that were slow to pay or had poor communication. How can I find companies that have a good reputation, positive feedback, and a high rating from previous employees to avoid similar issues?"

Color Schema

The chosen color scheme is designed to convey professionalism, trust, and a modern feel. It consists of a deep blue for a solid foundation, a light gray for a clean background, and a bright green for action-oriented elements to evoke growth and positivity.

  • Primary Color: Deep Blue (#2D3A5D)

    Usage: Headings, navigation bars, important text, and primary branding elements.

  • Secondary Color: Light Gray (#F8F9FA)

    Usage: Page background, form backgrounds, and card containers.

  • Accent Color 1: Bright Green (#4CAF50)

    Usage: Buttons, links, call-to-action elements, and positive rating indicators.

  • Accent Color 2: Orange (#E67E22)

    Usage: Used sparingly for secondary buttons or alerts to draw attention.

Typography

A combination of two fonts is selected to ensure readability and a professional appearance. A bold, modern font is used for emphasis, while a clean, classic sans-serif is used for body text.

  • Headings (H1, H2, etc.): Montserrat (from Google Fonts) - A bold and modern sans-serif font for strong visual hierarchy.
  • Body Text (Paragraphs, Lists, etc.): Lato (from Google Fonts) - A clean and highly readable sans-serif font for all other content.
